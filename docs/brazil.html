Brazil: A Country of Diversity and Wonder

Brazil is the largest country in both South America and the Latin American region, known for its vibrant culture, stunning beaches, and breathtaking natural beauty. From the iconic Christ the Redeemer statue in Rio de Janeiro to the majestic Iguazu Falls, Brazil has something to offer for every kind of traveler.

Attractions

Christ the Redeemer

Located in Rio de Janeiro, this iconic Art Deco statue of Jesus Christ is one of the Seven Wonders of the World and a symbol of Christianity.

Coordinates: -22.9068, -43.2105

Iguazu Falls

Located on the border of Brazil and Argentina, these breathtaking waterfalls are a sight to behold, with over 275 individual falls spanning almost 2 miles.

Coordinates: -25.6953, -54.4367

Amazon Rainforest

The largest tropical rainforest in the world, covering over 2 million square miles, the Amazon is home to an incredible array of plant and animal species.

Coordinates: -3.1333, -62.0667

Copacabana Beach

One of the most famous beaches in the world, Copacabana is a vibrant and bustling spot in Rio de Janeiro, known for its stunning white sand and crystal-clear waters.

Coordinates: -22.9673, -43.1823

Pão de Açúcar (Sugarloaf Mountain)

Located in Rio de Janeiro, this iconic mountain offers breathtaking views of the city and is a popular spot for hiking and rock climbing.

Coordinates: -22.9061, -43.1694

Lençóis Maranhenses National Park

Located in the state of Maranhão, this unique park is known for its stunning sand dunes and freshwater lagoons, creating a surreal landscape.

Coordinates: -2.9833, -43.1333
